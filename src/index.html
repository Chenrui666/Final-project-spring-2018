The H1B Visa permissions in 2016

Chart Reading Tips:



The outer donut chart shows the candidates distribution by state, while the inner donut chart shows it by job category. Hovering over the states' slices could show the job types inside that particular state.

The label in the middle is the hovered slice context, and the number under it is the amount of candidates for that category.

The H-1B is an employment-based, non-immigrant visa category for temporary foreign workers in the United States. For a foreign national to apply for H1-B visa, a US employer must offer a job and petition for H-1B visa with the US immigration department. This is the most common visa status applied for and held by international students once they complete college/ higher education (Masters, Ph.D.) and work in a full-time position. There are more than 65,000 candidates applied for H1B Visa in 2016. (Data source)

The visualization above shows the work states in the outer donut chart sorted descending while the occupation distributed in the inner donut chart, descending and clockwise. The states which have less than 100 candidates are put into "Other" category, same with occupations.
